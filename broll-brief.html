B-Roll Shot List

Videographer brief for website video footage

Project Overview

We're filming B-roll footage for two videos on the SkyeTrain website. Both videos feature Skye talking to camera (filmed separately), with B-roll cuts throughout. This shoot is purely the B-roll. No dialogue, no audio capture needed. Just clean visuals.

Video 1: Short intro (~45-60 sec total). Quick cuts of training footage and the gym.
Video 2: Assessment walkthrough (~3.5-4 min total). Shows what a first session with Skye looks like: consultation, movement assessment, coaching.

The footage should feel authentic and professional. Real coaching, real movement. Nothing staged or performative.

All shots at a glance

  • Pre + post session briefings in PT office [A1, A9]
  • Squat, bench and/or barbell [A2]
  • Elevated deadlift [A3]
  • DB shoulder press or incline DB chest press [A4]
  • Seated row [A5]
  • Scapula pull cue on seated row, Skye coaching [A6]
  • Lunge [A7]
  • Tricep pushdown, Skye demonstrating [A8]
  • Heart rate recovery test, treadmill or bike [A10]
  • Everfit app on phone screen [H4]
  • Establishing shot of gym floor [H5]
  • Client training independently [H6]

Shoot Details

LocationAnytime Fitness Prahran
TalentSkye + two clients (one male, one female). One primary client appears in most shots. Second client for movement variety.
Wardrobe (Skye)Training gear, same outfit for entire shoot.
Wardrobe (Clients)Clean gym gear, no competing logos.
AudioNot required. All B-roll is silent. Music and voiceover added in post.
Resolution4K preferred (allows cropping in post if needed).
Depth of fieldShallow throughout. Subject sharp, background soft. On a traditional camera, use a wide aperture (low f-number). On iPhone 17 Pro, use Cinematic Mode. Keeps focus on the movement and coaching, gives the footage a cinematic quality, and naturally blurs any gym members in the background while maintaining a sense of activity in the environment.
Key ruleEvery shot filmed twice: once wide (context, environment) and once close-up (movement, detail, action).
